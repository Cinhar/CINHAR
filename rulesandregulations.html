Rules and Regulations

Clinical Imaging Network for Health Awareness and Research (CINHAR)

1. Role of CINHAR as an Implementing Agency:

o CINHAR acts as a nonprofit implementing agency focused on advancing healthcare and research for the benefit of the community. Its primary function is to implement and manage projects that provide health and research benefits directly to the community through collaborations with academic and clinical institutions.

2. International Funding:

o CINHAR receives funds from international collaborations, especially from universities. However, CINHAR is not the direct recipient of the entire grant. Only the portion of the funds allocated for CINHAR’s role as the implementing agency will be transferred to CINHAR through the collaborating University.

o Upon award of the Grant, the Funding agency will release the funds to the collaborating university. The PI of the collaborating University will then allocate the necessary amount to CINHAR based on the budgetary allocation as per the Clinical Study Agreement (CSA) with the partnering Hospital/Clinical Center. These funds are used for project implementation, management, and proper functioning as per the CSA with the partnering Hospital/ Clinical Center.

3. Participating Hospital/ Clinical Center and MOUs:

o Participating Hospital/ Clinical Center that collaborate with CINHAR will have MOU specifically for a project to be implemented along with a detailed CSA. The MoUs will outline the details of the project, collaborating institutions, Data transfer policies, ethical guidelines and any non-disclosure and proprietary rights of CINHAR and the collaborating Hospital/Center. The MoU will also outline the terms and mechanisms of payments to be made by CINHAR for the clinical examinations, Scans and Reporting and the Consultancy Charges if any.

o The CSA will provide detailed information on the technical and clinical aspects of the study and the specific budgetary requirements to be projected in the proposal to be submitted to the Collaborating University.

o Hospitals/ Clinical Center may collaborate with CINHAR by signing of MoU and CSA, ensuring proper execution and governance of the research or healthcare projects.

o Both CINHAR and the partnering Hospital/Center are expected to contribute expertise, resources, or data, depending on the nature of the project and outlined in.

4. Requirements for Participating Scientists or Medical Doctors as Consultants:

o Any scientist or medical doctor wishing to become a clinical or technical consultant for the project involving CINHAR shall have a clear-cut definition of their proposed contributions and service to the project and shall be projected in the MoU.

o Consultants must align their work with CINHAR's mission of advancing health and research for community benefit.

o The regulations will specify the criteria and guidelines for appointing consultants, ensuring that their qualifications and expertise match the objectives of the specific projects.

5. Benefits from Imaging Data:

o CINHAR projects involving imaging data must ensure that findings or benefits derived from the data serve the community in two ways:

  1. Income-based benefits: The patients recruited by the participating Hospital/Center will receive the benefits of complimentary scans and clinical fee exemption as outlined in the CSA.
  2. Research-based benefits: The findings from the project should lead to actionable healthcare improvements for the community.

6. Consideration of Projects by CINHAR:

If a technical expert with ideas for clinical imaging studies seeks to collaborate with CINHAR, the organization can facilitate such partnerships, provided the proposal aligns with its mission. CINHAR's focus is on utilizing imaging data from community scans funded by research grants, with the ultimate goal of enhancing health awareness and supporting community well-being. By aligning proposals with these objectives, CINHAR fosters collaborations that leverage advanced imaging techniques to improve public health and drive scientific progress.

  • Proposal Submission: Any institution or individual interested in collaborating with CINHAR must submit a proposal plan in the format specified in the Project Request Form (PRF), which can be downloaded from the CINHAR website at https://cinhar.github.io/
  • Evaluation: The completed PRF should be emailed to the President of CINHAR, with contact details provided on the website. Each proposal will be evaluated by the CINHAR governing body to ensure alignment with the society’s mission and vision. Proposals that do not align with CINHAR’s objectives will not proceed to further stages of consideration.
  • Institutional Endorsement: Individuals seeking collaboration must provide a letter from the head of their parent institution, outlining the terms and conditions of their involvement in the project.
  • Application Review Process: The CINHAR Governing Body will review the application to assess the applicant’s suitability for the role of Investigator or Consultant. Applicants are required to email the following documents with the subject line “Project Collaboration Request”:
    • Curriculum Vitae (CV).
    • A letter from the parent institution confirming the individual’s current employment status, whether on contract or permanent basis.
    • A duly completed Project Request Form (PRF).
    • A formal letter from the applicant’s collaborating PI affiliated to a University outside or within India (Letter of Academic Support (LAS)). The letter of academic support should outline: 1) the nature of the collaboration between the applicant and the academic expert, 2) the specific contributions of each partner to the project, and 3) a brief justification for involving CINHAR as the implementing agency, highlighting how the project aligns with its not-for-profit objectives.
  • Upon acceptance of the proposal, CINHAR will identify the appropriate hospitals or centers to implement the project by facilitating a MoU and CSA between the applicant, CINHAR, and the clinical centers. The MoU and CSA may also be used by the applicant, in collaboration with their academic partner, to submit proposals to national or international funding agencies, provided CINHAR’s role as the implementing agency for community-level clinical imaging activities is clearly highlighted. All project costs outlined in the MoU and CSA should be incorporated into the proposal budget as part of the expenses incurred in conducting the community-based clinical study at the designated hospitals or centers.
  • A CINHAR representative will initiate the process of obtaining ethical clearance for the project approved by CINHAR from the respective ethics committees of the partnering centers/hospitals.

These steps ensure that all collaborators are thoroughly vetted and align with CINHAR’s standards and objectives.

7. Manpower and Recruitments:

All CINHAR manpower associated with a project is categorized either as consultants (PI/Co-PI) or as staff recruited for the implementation of scans and data analysis at each partnering center. The consultants (PI/Co-PI) are designated as outlined in the Project MoU/CSA, while the number and designation of staff and consultants are specified within the same documents. Consultants (PI/Co-PI) formally become part of the project manpower only once the project, approved by CINHAR, receives funding consideration from a sponsoring agency and the appropriate funds are released either by the partnering university or directly by the sponsoring agency.

  • Recruitment of Project Coordinators and Data Analysts: The recruitment of project coordinators and data analysts for each center will be conducted by a committee appointed by the CINHAR Governing Body. This committee will include at least two members in addition to the Consultants, Principal Investigators (PIs), or Co-Principal Investigators (Co-PIs) of the project. If external members are not available, these roles may be filled by members of the Governing Body.
  • Consultant and PI Remuneration: Remuneration for services provided by Consultants, PIs, and Co-PIs representing CINHAR or the partnering Hospitals/Centers, will be as specified in the Budgetary requirements outlined in the MoU/CSA. Payments are subject to the release of funds by the International/National/Private funding agency and must be approved by the project PI from the collaborating university or the funding agency, in accordance with the financial regulations of the collaborating university for disbursing such payments to CINHAR. CINHAR is obligated to release the salaries of Consultants, PIs, and Co-PIs strictly in accordance with the budgetary guidelines outlined in the MoU. Payments will be made through banking instruments authorized by the CINHAR governing body and transferred directly to the individual’s account if they are freelancing. However, if the individual is a regular or contract employee of their parent institution, CINHAR will release the salary or consultancy fee to the parent institution on behalf of the individual. Exceptions to this policy may be granted only if CINHAR receives prior approval from the head of the parent institution, explicitly providing a no-objection declaration for direct payment to the individual.
  • Allocation of Project Roles and Remuneration: Roles such as project coordination or clinical data analysis will be assigned based on the funding received. Payments to such staff will be disbursed directly through CINHAR through payment authorization forms, or Cheques duly endorsed by the Secretary and President of CINHAR.
  • Fund Allocation: Project Funds from collaborating universities, will be credited directly to the Bank account of CINHAR prior to the release of salaries.

8. CINHAR Governing Body:

  • All project proposals, collaborations, and consultant appointments must be reviewed and approved by CINHAR’s governing body. The governing body is responsible for ensuring that all activities align with CINHAR’s mission and bylaws.
  • The structure of the governing body memberships is categorized as honorary and scientific memberships. The honorary members shall be senior members of society with leading roles in societal, administrative, legal and financial realms. There shall be at least one honorary member from each realm. At least four scientific members shall be leading technical experts in Medical Physics, MRI, Medical Imaging and Medical Electronics and at least four scientific members shall be leading clinical experts in Neurology, radiology, Cardiology and Psychiatry.
  • The governing body will nominate one of the members to act as the CINHAR representative for a project. The nominated member will be responsible for initiating the MoU, CSA and ethics clearance activities of the project on behalf of CINHAR. If the project is proposed by one of the scientific members, the governing body will nominate that member to take the role of CINHAR representative for the project.
  • The society operates in accordance with established bylaws, which define the governance structure, roles and responsibilities of the governing body, and regulations regarding project funding, consultant appointments, and remuneration.
  • The Governing Body shall consist of the President, Secretary, Treasurer, and up to twelve members. The President, Secretary, and Treasurer positions are nominated from among the members of the Governing body.
  • The term of office for the Governing Body members shall be Five years.
  • Nominations for the members can be considered anytime during the year and ratified during the Annual General Meeting (AGM) held in the month of January every calendar year.

9. Powers and Duties of the Governing Body:

  • Formulate policies, plan activities, and oversee the functioning of the Society.
  • Manage funds, assets, and property of the Society.
  • Approve budgets, authorize expenditures, and ensure financial transparency.
  • Appoint committees and delegate tasks to fulfill the Society’s objectives.

10. Meetings:

  • The Society shall hold an Annual General Meeting (AGM) every year to discuss and approve the annual report, accounts, and elect the Governing Body.
  • Extraordinary General Meetings (EGM) can be called as required by the Governing Body or on the written request of at least one-third of the members.
  • Notice of meetings must be sent 21 days in advance.
  • The meetings can be online based on a set agenda shared with the members 21 days in advance.
  • Members are allowed to share their views/decisions on the set agenda through emails, and can be considered equivalent to their participation in the meetings.
  • Electronic signature of the minutes of the meetings is acceptable.

11. Finance and Accounts:

  • The funds of the Society shall be derived from donations, grants, subscriptions, and other sources approved by the Governing Body.
  • The accounts shall be audited annually by a qualified auditor appointed by the AGM.
  • The Treasurer shall maintain proper records of all financial transactions and present financial statements at the AGM.

12. Amendment of Rules:

  • Any amendment to the Rules and Regulations shall require a two-thirds majority vote at the AGM or EGM.
  • Proposed amendments must be circulated to members at least 30 days before the meeting.

13. Dissolution:

  • The Society may be dissolved by a three-fourths majority vote of the members present at an EGM called for this purpose.
  • Upon dissolution, the assets of the Society shall be transferred to another similar Society as decided by the Governing Body.

14. Miscellaneous:

  • All matters not covered by these Rules and Regulations shall be governed by the provisions of the Societies Registration Act, 1860.

15. Signature of Founding Members:

  • The Rules and Regulations are signed by the following founding members:
  • - President
  • - Secretary
  • - Treasurer

16. Financial Management and Remuneration Guidelines:

1. Ad hoc Expenditure

  • Ad-hoc Payments: Payments made as needed for temporary or specific tasks.
  • Auditor Fee: Compensation for financial auditing services.

2. Honorarium and Sitting Fee

A sitting fee, capped at ₹2500 per member, is allowed for physical meetings. However, there is no sitting fee for online meetings.

3. Financial Auditing and Bookkeeping

The financial auditing and bookkeeping tasks will be delegated to an external party, selected by the governing body. The charges for these services will be handled through ad-hoc payments as required and ratified by the governing body.

4. Office Maintenance and Rental Charges

Office maintenance and rental expenses, borne by the society and affiliated imaging centers, will be covered by the overhead component of the grant.

5. Project Payments

Project-related payments include remunerations to Principal Investigators (PIs), Co-Principal Investigators (Co-PIs), and technical personnel, as outlined in the awarded grant proposal.

The remuneration of the PI and Co-PIs shall not exceed ₹2,00,000 per month or ₹20,00,000 annually, depending on the payment structure.

o All payment should meet the terms and guidelines as stationed in the funding agency with ratification of the governing body.

6. Payment Terms and Intervals

o Payments will follow the stipulations outlined in the grant proposal or, if missing, will adhere to the consensus of the governing body.

7. Personnel Requirements for Affiliated Centers

o Each affiliated center must include the following personnel:

1. Project Coordinator

Roles and Responsibilities:

• Project Planning and Execution:

o Develop and manage project timelines, deliverables, and milestones.

o Coordinate with the Principal Investigator (PI), Co-PIs, and technical staff to ensure that project goals and objectives are met.

o Oversee the project lifecycle, from initiation through completion, ensuring alignment with the grant proposal and organizational goals.

• Communication and Coordination:

o Act as the main communication link between the various stakeholders, including researchers, sponsors, governing body, and affiliated centers.

o Schedule and lead regular project meetings, preparing agendas and documenting minutes for the team and stakeholders.

o Ensure clear communication between technical and non-technical teams.

• Budget and Resource Management:

o Monitor project budget and ensure that expenditures align with the approved financial plan.

o Prepare regular financial reports and ensure that resources (including personnel and equipment) are allocated efficiently.

o Collaborate with the finance team to process payments for project-related activities, ensuring compliance with the financial guidelines of the grant.

• Documentation and Reporting:

o Maintain accurate project records and documentation, ensuring all updates are logged and accessible.

o Prepare periodic progress reports for the funding agency and the governing body.

o Ensure the completion of all administrative tasks related to the project, including compliance with ethical and legal standards.

• Risk Management:

o Identify potential risks that may affect project timelines, deliverables, or budget.

o Develop risk mitigation strategies and adjust the project plan as needed.

Qualifications and Experience:

• Minimum Qualification:

o B-Tech in Engineering, Computer Science, Biomedical Engineering, or a related field.

• Preferred Qualification:

o MSc in Project Management, Biomedical Engineering, or a relevant scientific discipline.

• Experience:

o Minimum of 3-5 years of experience in project management, preferably in research or clinical settings.

o Experience managing large-scale scientific or clinical research projects is highly desirable.

• Skills:

o Strong leadership and organizational skills.

o Excellent communication and coordination abilities.

o Proficiency in project management software (e.g., Microsoft Project, Asana).

o Understanding of financial management in grant-funded projects.

o Knowledge of research protocols and compliance.

2. Data Analyst

Roles and Responsibilities:

• Data Collection and Management:

o Gather, organize, and manage large datasets from various sources, including clinical trials, imaging data, and research studies.

o Collaborate with researchers and technical staff to ensure accurate data collection protocols.

o Maintain and update databases in compliance with organizational and research standards.

• Data Analysis:

o Perform statistical analyses on collected data to identify trends, patterns, and significant results.

o Apply advanced data processing techniques such as machine learning, regression analysis, and clustering methods for in-depth analysis of clinical data.

o Prepare data reports and visualizations to support research findings and publications.

• Technical Assistance and Support:

o Provide analytical support to PIs, Co-PIs, and other researchers, assisting in the interpretation of data and the development of conclusions.

o Work closely with software engineers and biostatisticians to implement data-driven solutions in ongoing projects.

o Assist in preparing data for publications, presentations, and project reports.

• Quality Control and Assurance:

o Ensure data accuracy and consistency by conducting data validation and integrity checks.

o Monitor data quality throughout the project, ensuring compliance with data governance and security protocols.

o Conduct regular audits of datasets to ensure they meet the required standards for research integrity.

• Collaboration and Communication:

o Work in collaboration with the Project Coordinator to ensure that project timelines for data-related tasks are met.

o Present analysis findings to research teams and stakeholders in a clear and understandable manner.

o Contribute to the design of data collection tools and methodologies to improve project efficiency.

Qualifications and Experience:

• Minimum Qualification:

o B-Tech, MSc in Data Science, Information Technology, or related fields.

• Preferred Qualification:

o MSc in Data Science, Biostatistics, or a related discipline.

• Experience:

o 2-4 years of experience in data analysis, preferably within the healthcare or research sectors.

o Experience in handling complex datasets related to clinical imaging or biomedical research is highly desirable.

• Skills:

o Proficiency in statistical software (e.g., SPSS, SAS, R, Python).

o Experience with database management systems (e.g., SQL) and data visualization tools (e.g., Tableau, Power BI).

o Strong analytical skills with a deep understanding of statistical methods.

o Excellent communication skills for presenting complex data in an accessible manner.

o Familiarity with clinical trial data and imaging technologies would be a plus.

8. Remuneration Limits

o The maximum remuneration for PIs and Co-PIs shall not exceed ₹2,00,000 per month or ₹20,00,000 annually.

9. Payment Compliance

o All payments must align with the terms set forth by the funding agency and require ratification by the governing body. Payments shall not exceed the limits specified above.

10. Income Tax Deductions

• Income tax will be deducted at the source in compliance with the PI or Co-PI's contractual agreement, considering other regular payments declared.

11. Transaction Process

• All transactions will be made directly through the society’s bank account, with prior approval of the governing body during the contractual agreement signing.

12. Tax Deducted at Source (TDS)

• TDS for each payment will be audited by the financial authority of the society, as designated by the governing body.
